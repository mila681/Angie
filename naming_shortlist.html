Naming Exploration
How to read this

A name is an invitation.

It gets spoken aloud to a friend, hashtagged on unboxings, whispered on the inside of a box lid. It has to hold all of that.

These ten names span four feelings. Each is scored on four dimensions — availability, scalability, memorability, brand fit — out of 20 total. The scores are a professional anchor, not a verdict. Your gut can override anything.

How to read each name

One page. One name. Top to bottom.

The score tells you where it lands professionally. The essence line is the name in one sentence. Meaning & fit explain the why. Heard aloud shows how it reads in the world. Strengths & watch-outs are the tradeoffs.

The four dimensions, explained
Availability — how clean is the trademark, domain, handle landscape
Scalability — can the name stretch from box to full brand house
Memorability — how sticky after one hearing; retellable
Brand fit — how well it holds the velvet-ritual-cycle feeling

I
Duskmere
dusk · meer
Score
19/20
Avail
Scale
Memory
Fit
A still pool at evening. A place to step into when the world has gone quiet.
Meaning

Dusk is the soft hour between day and night. Mere is old English for a still body of water — a pool, a lake (as in Windermere).

Why it fits

The name is the velvet temple. Held, dim, warm, water-quiet. Doesn't announce cycle — holds everything you'll ever build.

"I took myself to Duskmere this week."
Strengths
  • Wide open in trademark space
  • Scales from box to lifestyle brand
  • Old-feeling, but reads new
Worth watching
  • "-mere" may read British or fantasy at first
  • Needs confident branding to settle the sound

A place of softness. A workshop of gentleness you step into.
Meaning

Suede + -ery (as in perfumery, bindery, rookery). The suffix means "a place where this happens."

Why it fits

The word itself performs the brand promise — you feel softness reading it. Works as "The Suedery" or "Suedery & Co."

"A month of gentleness, kept in The Suedery."
Strengths
  • Appears completely open
  • Tactile and memorable
  • Works across products beautifully
Worth watching
  • Leans texture-first, may read skincare more than cycle
  • Category needs the tagline to surface

Questions to Sit With

How to choose.

Not a formula — but five questions that help founders pick names they can live with for ten years.

Which one made you feel something when you said it aloud?

Names are sound before they are meaning. The one that sits well in your mouth is the one your customer will say easily too.

Which one do you want to look at on a label every day for ten years?

You'll write this name thousands of times. Pick the one still alive on the thousandth.

Do you want the name to explain itself, or to become meaningful through you?

Duskmere, Suedery, Madder Bloom, Crimson Ivy, Hers Truly do some explaining on their own. Reliqua, Seraline, Vespera, Vesper, Carmine leave more room for you to fill in.

Which name is a house big enough for everything you'll build inside it?

The box is the start. An app, an oil line, a retreat, a community all live inside one name. Which one can hold them all?

Which register do you want to live in?

Soft & textural invites quietly. Sacred & celestial asks her to slow down. Intimate & letter-like is a love note. Bold & botanical claims the space.
